Learn about Cookies

Cookies are like small notes that websites leave on your computer or phone when you visit them. These notes tell the website what you like and what you have done on the website before. Some cookies stay until you close your internet, and others stay longer. Cookies can help make websites better, but they can also be used to track what you do online. You can choose to block or delete cookies if you want. There are rules about using cookies, and some websites have to ask if you want them to use cookies.

Three points about Local Storage vs Session Storage

Local Storage stays on your computer even after you close the website, but Session Storage goes away when you close the website.

Local Storage can be used on all parts of a website, but Session Storage can only be used on the part you are using right now.

Local Storage usually has a higher storage limit than Session Storage, which means that you can store more data in Local Storage than you can in Session Storage.
